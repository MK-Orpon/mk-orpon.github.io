# Student Leadership Development Workshop (SLDW)

**Type:** Project / Volunteer Work  
**Organization:** Bangladesh Center for Communication Programs (BCCP)  
**Location:** Chattogram, Bangladesh  
**Duration:** 01 March 2023 – 10 June 2023  
**Position/Role:** Team Member  
**Type of Organization:** NGO  

## Project Description
The Student Leadership Development Workshops (SLDW), implemented by BCCP, is a youth leadership initiative designed to empower university students with leadership skills, civic responsibility, and values of peace, tolerance, and democratic engagement.  

The program engages students from public and private universities across multiple divisions of Bangladesh, including Chattogram, through structured workshops, community assignments, and student–police collaboration. Participants are equipped with life skills such as critical thinking, problem-solving, conflict resolution, and community organizing, enabling them to address social issues including child protection, peacebuilding, and prevention of violent extremism.  

Through community outreach, peer learning, and civic engagement activities, SLDW has strengthened youth leadership, promoted social cohesion, and fostered sustainable student–community–police partnerships contributing to peace and social development in Bangladesh.

## Key Objectives
- Strengthen youth leadership and civic responsibility  
- Raise awareness on child protection and prevention of abuse  
- Promote student–community–police collaboration for peacebuilding  

## Description of Major Activities
- Contributed to raising awareness on child abuse and child protection through community-based initiatives  
- Supported multi-faceted awareness campaign at CRB, Chattogram, including leaflet distribution, banner displays, focus group discussions, and public interviews  
- Facilitated a civic education session for children at a local slum school in collaboration with law enforcement  
- Engaged with the Chattogram Metropolitan Police to strengthen student–police–community collaboration  
- Conducted interviews with professors, teachers, and police officers to improve outreach strategies and community engagement  

## Outcomes & Impact
- Increased awareness on child abuse and child rights in Chattogram communities  
- Strengthened student–police–community collaboration  
- Enhanced leadership capacity, civic engagement, and democratic values among youth
